What am I looking at?
Each time the investigator agent finishes a work session, it saves a complete copy of its memory — every hypothesis, finding, piece of evidence, and suspect score — from CockroachDB to Amazon S3. Each of those saved copies is one frame below. Drag the slider to move backward and forward through the investigation's history and watch the agent's thinking evolve:
  • The counters show how much the agent had discovered by that point in time (hypotheses it was pursuing, findings it recorded, evidence it collected).
  • Each bar is one suspect; its length and number are that person's guilt score at that moment. Watch scores rise as evidence accumulates.
  • A green-outlined row is a suspect who first appears in this frame — a new lead the agent just uncovered.
  • Hover a row to read the agent's reasoning recorded at that time.
This is only possible because the agent's memory persists in CockroachDB. A stateless chatbot would have nothing to replay.
